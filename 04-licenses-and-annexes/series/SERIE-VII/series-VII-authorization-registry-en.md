# **SERIES VII — AUTHORIZATION REGISTRY**

*(Confidential Document — Guardian Only)*

# **0. Nature and Purpose**

The Authorization Registry is the internal document that records:

* all decisions made by the Guardian,
* structural reasoning behind each decision,
* associated Field reading,
* risk analysis,
* expected impact,
* date,
* and classification.

It ensures:

* traceability,
* legal protection,
* systemic continuity,
* precedent tracking,
* and stability of the Guardian’s function.

---

# **1. Official Registry Format**

Each entry must follow:

```
ID: [unique code: year-month-day-number]
Date: [mm/dd/yyyy]
Requester: [name, institution, email]
Request Type:
    - Commercial Use
    - Educational Use
    - Derivative Work
    - Technical Integration
    - Automation
    - Translation
    - Research
    - Other
Summary of Request:
[text]

Legal Analysis (relevant License section):
[text]

Structural Analysis (PER, LCV, MHA, Structural Core):
[text]

Field Reading:
[text]

Future Impact Analysis:
[text]

Decision:
    - Approved
    - Approved with Conditions
    - Denied

Justification:
[text]

Conditions (if applicable):
[text]

Guardian Record:
[text signature]
```

---

# **2. Request Classification**

### **Category A — No Risk**

Simple uses with no structural impact.

### **Category B — Medium Risk**

Touches peripheral structures but not the Core.

### **Category C — High Risk**

Affects internal frameworks or conceptual architecture.

### **Category D — Existential Risk**

Attempts to replicate, replace or distort LICHTARA.

---

# **3. Evaluation Criteria**

The Guardian must assess:

* potential distortion,
* risk of erosion,
* replication risk,
* vibrational impact,
* public confusion potential,
* authority displacement risk,
* institutional risk.

---

# **4. Transparency Policy**

This document is **confidential**.

Anonymous summaries may appear in:

* reports,
* version histories,
* institutional communications,

but **never** the internal reasoning of the Guardian.

---

# **5. Internal Audit**

The Guardian must:

* review the Registry at every new version cycle (v5, v6…),
* observe patterns in requests,
* identify recurring risks,
* adjust Series V when needed.
